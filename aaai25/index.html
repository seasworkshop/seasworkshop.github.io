About SEAS 2025

As the AI community progresses in crafting algorithms with human-like capabilities, it is essential to understand the implications of these advancements for scalable and efficient AI systems. For instance, AI systems are known to efficiently handle small-scale data tasks, but managing them for large-scale, dynamically growing datasets presents different and new challenges. To address these challenges, researchers from both academia and industry must come together and make advancements in fundamental research and applied technologies. The organizing committee, program committee, and keynote speakers of SEAS consist of experts from both academia and industry with rich experiences in designing and developing robust computer vision (CV), machine learning (ML) and artificial intelligence (AI) algorithms and transitioning these technologies into real-world solutions. SEAS provides a unique platform for researchers to better understand and tackle the scalability and efficiency issues in CV, ML, and AI.

Call for Papers

We welcome full paper submissions (up to 7 pages, excluding references or supplementary materials). Please submit at the following CMT website: SEAS 2025 CMT submission website. 

The paper submissions must be in pdf format and use the AAAI 2025 official templates. All submissions must be anonymous and conform to the AAAI 2025 standards for double-blind review. The accepted papers will be posted on the workshop website and will not appear in the AAAI 2025 official proceedings. Since AAAI 2025 does not provide the official workshop proceedings, we plan to work with our past publisher partners (e.g. Springer, MDPI, etc.) to publish the accepted papers. At least one author of each accepted submission must present the paper at the workshop in person. Otherwise, the paper will be removed from the workshop program.  

Submission deadline: November 17, 2024 AOE Time  
Notification to authors: December 11, 2024
Camera ready deadline:  December 18, 2024 AOE Time

We invite the submission of original and high-quality research papers in the topics related to scalable and efficient AI systems. Accepted work will be presented as either an oral, a spotlight, or a poster presentation. 

Topics of Interest

The topics for SEAS 2025 include, but are not limited to:

  • Algorithms and theories for efficient model training and operational scalability.
  • Sector-specific designs for scalable AI, such as telecom, e-commerce, medical imaging, etc.
  • Strategies for managing scalable training when new data is integrated.
  • Analysis of AI system performance as data scales up.
  • Cross-field research insights to improve data handling in scalable AI systems.
  • Methods for ensuring robust performance despite rapid data growth.
  • Incremental and online learning strategies for real-time data adaptation.
  • Innovative post-training approaches to make efficient and scalable AI models.
